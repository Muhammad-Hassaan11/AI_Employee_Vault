We let our AI Employee draft the email. We never let it hit send.

That's the rule behind every automation we build: the AI can read, sort, draft, and prepare — but anything that leaves the building (an email, a social post, a payment) sits in an approval queue until a human signs off.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

→ Incoming emails get triaged and, if needed, a reply gets drafted automatically
→ The draft lands in a review queue with full context — not just "approve/reject" on faith
→ Nothing sends, posts, or pays out until a person flips it to approved
→ Every action, approved or not, gets logged for a full audit trail

It's slower than letting the AI run wild. It's also the only version of this that a business owner can actually trust.

The point of automation isn't removing humans from the loop — it's putting them at the one point that actually matters: the decision, not the busywork.
