Most small businesses think machine learning is out of reach. It isn't.

Machine learning is just software that gets better at a task the more examples it sees — instead of following rules a human wrote, it learns the pattern from your own data.

Here's where it quietly earns its keep for a small business:

→ Sorting incoming emails by intent, so the urgent ones surface first
→ Predicting which leads are most likely to convert, so you spend time where it counts
→ Flagging unusual expenses before they become a problem
→ Drafting replies that sound like you, trained on how you actually write

You don't need a data science team. You need a clear task, a bit of clean data, and a system that keeps a human in the loop for every decision that matters.

The businesses winning with ML in 2026 aren't the ones with the biggest models. They're the ones who pointed a simple model at a real, repetitive problem — and let it compound.

What's one repetitive task in your business you'd hand to a machine that learns?
